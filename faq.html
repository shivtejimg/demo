As per the new guidelines of NATIONAL MEDICAL COMMISSION ( NMC ), students doing MBBS from INDIA or ABROAD are on same platform as all have to clear the Next exam after completion of their degree, therefore most of the students have inclination towards abroad for their further studies.
As per the NMC guideline, qualifying NEET examination is the minimum requirement to study MBBS abroad as well as in INDIA .The scorecard of NEET will be considered as license for NExT examination conducted by NMC.
All the universities abroad which are approved by MCI are regarded as good for MBBS. Although the best countries for MBBS which are more suitable for Indian students are RUSSIA , KYRGYZSTAN, KAZAKHSTAN, GEORGIA, UZBEKISTAN.
There are very few documents required for admission into any university ;
  1. Valid Indian Passport.
  2. NEET Scorecard with Minimum qualifying marks in the required category.
  3. Cast Certiﬁcate (if required).
  4. 2-8 Passport Size Photograph.
  5. 10th and 12th Marksheet.
  6. HIV- AIDS negative report {in some universities it is Mandatory}.
  7. Transfer Certiﬁcate.
At the time of admission the minimum age of student should be 17 years. Is an MBBS degree from Abroad recognised in India ? YES, degrees from the universities which are veriﬁed and recognised by MEDICAL COUNCIL OF INDIA (MCI) are valid in INDIA.
Before there were no such guidelines but from 2023 onwards students doing MBBS course should and must complete the duration of at least for 54 months, along with an internship period of a minimum of 12 months in the same medical institutions.
Depending upon the country the student is choosing. In countries like Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan,Uzbekistan the duration is 5 years. However, in Russia and Georgia it will be for 6 years.
YES, it is mandatory for students pursuing MBBS from INDIA OR ABROAD to qualify NExT exam with minimum qualifying marks then only they will allow for their further internship and PG in India.
